# The Problem

**AI-generated images are now good enough to fool people - and courts.**

Two things are happening:

1. **Fake evidence is being submitted** - AI-generated photos presented as real
2. **Real evidence is being called fake** - the "deepfake defense" / "liar's dividend"

---

---

# Real Cases

### Mendones v. Cushman

> Plaintiffs submitted **9 deepfake videos and AI-altered images** as evidence.
> The judge dismissed the entire case.

### USA v. Reffitt

> A January 6 defendant's lawyer claimed real video evidence was a **"deepfake."**
> The judge pressed him - he couldn't back it up.

---

---

# The Gap

Existing detection tools were trained on **social media content**.

Law enforcement images look very different:

- Grainy bodycam footage
- Low-resolution surveillance cameras
- Compressed, blurry, poorly lit

**Nobody has tested whether detection tools work on this kind of content.**

That's what we're doing.

---

---

# What We're Building

### A Benchmark Dataset

- **10,000 real images** from public datasets (Open Images, COCO)
- **10,000 AI-generated images** using Stable Diffusion, Flux
- **3 quality levels:** clean, moderate degradation, heavy degradation

### A Tool Evaluation

- Test 5-6 existing detection tools against our dataset
- Measure accuracy, precision, recall across quality levels

### Deliverables

- Research paper
- Policy brief for NOBLE leadership
- Code repository (reproducible)
